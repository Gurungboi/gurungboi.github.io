# Copy Guide — Sunil Gurung Portfolio

## Purpose

Document the voice, tone, and copy conventions for the portfolio. This ensures consistency across all text content.

---

## 1. Voice Profile

### Core Identity

**Engineer who ships.** Not a "thought leader," not a "visionary." Someone who builds things that work, leads teams that deliver, and thinks carefully about tools.

### Voice Traits

| Trait | Expression | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Specific** | Numbers, names, concrete outcomes | "60% less duplicate code" not "significantly improved" |
| **Confident** | Direct statements, no hedging | "Led the delivery" not "helped with" |
| **Calm** | Measured, never breathless | "I ship things that matter" not "I absolutely crushed it!" |
| **Human** | First-person, relatable moments | "Straight out of my BSc" |
| **Honest** | Admits challenges when real | "State complexity caused subtle bugs" |

## 3. Words to Use / Avoid

### Use

- Ship, deliver, led, owned, drove
- Built, rebuilt, introduced, restructured
- Improved, reduced, cut, accelerated
- Critical thinking, thoughtful, careful
- Real, specific, concrete

### Avoid

- Rockstar, ninja, guru (cringe)
- Revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive (AI-generated feel)
- Innovative (overused to the point of meaning nothing)
- Passionate (show, don't say)
- Synergy (never)
- Leveraging (use "using" or "with")

## 4. Formatting Conventions

### Numbers

| Type | Format | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Percentages | Number + description | "60% less duplicate code" |
| Years | Number only when clear | "7 Years iOS Dev" |
| Time durations | Number + unit | "45s build time", "3+ minutes" |
| Money/stat | No currency unless relevant | "4× Promoted" |

### Company Names

- First mention: **Full company name** in bold
- Subsequent: plain text

### Job Titles

- Capitalize when specific role: Senior Mobile Engineer, Acting Tech Lead
- Use as descriptor: "team of four engineers" (lowercase when generic)

---

## 5. Special Copy Elements

### Pull Quotes

- Italic Playfair Display
- Red left border
- Reflect a genuine belief or approach
- Never generic wisdom

### Ticker Text

- ALL CAPS in DM Mono
- Skills and technologies only
- "◆" separator between items
- Duplicated for seamless loop

### Promotion Badges

- Chronological order (newest first)
- Current role highlighted in red
- Format: "Senior · Dec 2024 →"

### Availability Statement

- Green dot (animated blink)
- "Available" or "Available for hire"
- Location if relevant: "Sydney, NSW, Australia"

## 7. Section-Specific Notes

### Hero Bio

Keep it to 2-3 punchy sentences. Lead with: where you've been → what you do → what you care about.

Current:
> "From intern to Senior Engineer at Australia's largest gift card platform. iOS, Android, Kotlin Multiplatform, Apple Pay, and AI-assisted workflows. I ship things that matter."

### About Lede

This is your hook. 1-2 sentences that make someone keep reading. Current is strong — it establishes trajectory and tenure.

### Architecture Intro

One sentence that frames the section. Current is good:
> "I've driven iOS through three full architectural generations at Prezzee — not as academic exercises, but as direct responses to real scaling pain."

### Contact CTA

Short, confident, action-oriented. Current "Open to great work." is excellent.
